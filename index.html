European Theatre
Western & Eastern Fronts
Aug 23, 1939
Non-Aggression Pact (Molotov–Ribbentrop Pact)
Germany and the Soviet Union agree not to attack each other and secretly divide Eastern Europe, allowing Hitler to invade Poland.
Sept 1, 1939
Poland Invaded
Germany invades Poland using blitzkrieg tactics, beginning World War II in Europe.
May 26 – June 4, 1940
Dunkirk Evacuation
Allied soldiers trapped in northern France are evacuated by British ships across the English Channel.
July – Oct 1940
Battle of Britain
Germany launches massive air attacks on Britain but fails to gain air superiority.
June 22, 1941
Soviet Union Invaded (Operation Barbarossa)
Germany launches a massive invasion of the Soviet Union, opening the Eastern Front.
Aug 23, 1942
Stalingrad Attacks Ordered
Hitler orders German forces to capture Stalingrad, leading to one of the war's bloodiest battles.
Nov 8, 1942
Allies Land in North Africa (Operation Torch)
U.S. and British forces land in North Africa to push Axis forces out of the region.
Feb 2, 1943
German Surrender in Stalingrad
German 6th Army surrenders to the Soviets, marking a major turning point on the Eastern Front.
June 6, 1944
D-Day (Normandy Invasion)
Allied forces land in Normandy, France, opening a Western Front against Nazi Germany.
Dec 16, 1944 – Jan 25, 1945
Battle of the Bulge
Germany launches its last major offensive in western Europe through Belgium's Ardennes forest.
Apr 30, 1945
Hitler Commits Suicide
Adolf Hitler kills himself in Berlin as Soviet forces close in on the city.
May 8, 1945
Germany Surrenders (V-E Day)
Germany officially surrenders to the Allied powers, ending WWII in Europe.
🌏
Pacific Theatre
Asia & the Pacific Ocean
Dec 7, 1941
Pearl Harbor
Japan launches a surprise attack on the U.S. naval base in Hawaii, bringing the United States into WWII.
Feb 19, 1942
Japanese Internment Camps in America
President Roosevelt orders the relocation and internment of Japanese Americans during the war.
May 4–8, 1942
Battle of the Coral Sea
First naval battle fought entirely with aircraft carriers; Japan's advance toward Australia is stopped.
May 6, 1942
Allies Surrender in the Philippines
U.S. and Filipino forces surrender to Japan at Corregidor after months of fighting.
June 4–7, 1942
Battle of Midway
U.S. Navy defeats Japan and destroys four aircraft carriers, turning the tide in the Pacific.
Feb 7, 1943
Japan Abandons Guadalcanal
Japan withdraws from Guadalcanal after months of fighting, shifting momentum to the Allies in the Pacific.
1942–1945
Manhattan Project
Secret U.S. program to develop the atomic bomb, ultimately changing modern warfare.
Oct 23–26, 1944
Battle of Leyte Gulf
The largest naval battle in history; Allied forces defeat the Japanese navy in the Philippines.
Mar 26, 1945
Iwo Jima Captured
U.S. forces capture the island after intense fighting, gaining a strategic base near Japan.
June 22, 1945
Okinawa Captured
The U.S. secures Okinawa after a brutal battle, providing a staging ground for a potential invasion of Japan.
Aug 6 & 9, 1945
Hiroshima / Nagasaki Atomic Bombings
The U.S. drops atomic bombs on two Japanese cities, leading to Japan's surrender.
Aug 15, 1945
Emperor Hirohito Announces Surrender
Japan's emperor announces acceptance of Allied terms and the end of the war.
Sept 2, 1945
Japan Signs Official Surrender
Japan formally signs surrender documents aboard the USS Missouri, officially ending WWII.
